For the  term paper, you will write a paper exploring a philosophical argument pertaining to the impact of a sub-field of the Robotics and Machine Learning on the society.

 

Of course, there are many possible arguments to pursue for this assignment. The topic (argument) that you choose must be approved by the instructor. If you wish, you may talk to instructor about your topic, and/or turn in your draft before its due date for feedback.

Note that the argument of your paper concerns a sub-field of Robotics and Machine Learning. To provide support for your position, you are required to find a real-world system that is a representative of a system from the sub-field you chose. However, your paper's topic is not the real world system; rather the system should be discussed only as a representative of your chosen sub-field.

 

The first step is to choose the appropriate thesis / position concerning the impact of a sub-field Robotics and Machine Learning on the society. You have freedom to choose whatever sub-field you may find interesting. Using the list of resources provided below and/or your own resources, you will focus on a single sub-field of Robotics and Machine Learning for your term paper. For your chosen sub-field, you should then find a real-world system that is the representative system of the chosen sub-field, and investigate whether the chosen system supports your overall thesis. Some of the possible subfields of Robotics and Machine Learning are:

  • - Genetic Algorithms (Evolutionary Programming)
  • - Neural Networks
  • - Robot Navigation
  • - Planning
  • - Pattern Recognition
  • - Machine Learning in Computer Games
  • - Natural Language Understanding
  • - Industrial Robotics
  • - Military Robotics
  • - Game Playing (e.g., chess, checkers, etc.)
  • - Commercial Robotics
  • - Robotics Sports
  • - Robotics in the Space Program
  • - Robotics applications in medicine, science, and other fields
  • - And many, many other Robotics and Machine Learning techniques and issues

 

Once you have chosen a thesis for your paper, you must find at least 3 different articles about your topic. The articles should be narrowly focused to the topic of your paper, and not on the Robotics and Machine Learning in general. The articles you choose as your references must be at least 4 pages in length and from credible sources (no internet class reports or anything of that nature). One of the articles must come from a respectable journal. At most one of your sources may be an internet article.

 

The length of this paper will be between six and eight pages, written in 12 point Times New Roman font and double spaced with standard margins. The term paper will account for 12% of a student’s overall grade. Please follow the formatting instructions clearly, as points will be deducted for papers that are too short or papers that have different font type and size.

 

Among others, your paper must clearly address these issues:

 

- The position (thesis) you are taking about the impact of a particular subfield of Robotics and Machine Learning

- A brief history of the chosen sub-field and the achievements accomplished in the chosen sub-field (up to date)

- A clear description of your real-world system, and more importantly, how this system supports your overall thesis

- The potential future impact of the chosen sub-field and your argument on the society

 

The title, a draft ,and the references are due on November 14th. Your final submitted paper must not deviate from the topic you choose in your draft. The full paper is due on November 28th.

 

 

 

The following criteria will be used for paper evaluation:

Organization

If the paper is to be a good effort, it MUST be well organized. The best topic and brilliant scholarship can be destroyed by poor organization of material. Ask yourself the following questions:

  • Is there a logical progression of thought?
  • Are there significant gaps or missing parts?
  • Does the organization of the paper help or hinder the reader's flow?
  • Does each paragraph present a coherent theme?

Above all, make sure that your paper has an introduction, with a clearly stated thesis; along with a conclusion that summarizes the major points.

 

Presentation and Original Thought

Be sure to include your own thoughts regarding the sub-field discussed in the articles you choose. This is to show the reader that you have put some thought into the reference material and have come up with some conclusions of your own. Your paper must not simply be a summary of the articles you have read for this assignment.

 

Clarity

Is your paper professional, succinct and easy to read? Are key concepts highlighted? Was it easy to understand what the paper was about?

 

Content

  • a clearly stated, narrowly defined thesis concerning a sub-field of Robotics and Machine Learning
  • an introduction that gives adequate background for your topic
  • discussion of the history, present and future impact, achievements up to date, and a representative system of your chosen sub-field
  • description of your sources of information/data and how you are going to approach your topic
  • clear presentation of only RELEVANT information. Do not fill pages with information that has nothing to do with your paper topic.
  • how well did you integrate the different articles?
  • was the significance of the topic and the papers (or lack of!) made clear to the reader?
  • accuracy, originality of thought, and quality of expression
  • writing mechanics: spelling, clarity
  • DO NOT simply stop writing when you reach the size limit of the term paper! If the paper has not thoroughly dealt with the issues you set out to discuss, then it is incomplete.

 

Overall Quality of the Paper

  • what was the overall quality of the document? (this will be my judgment call)
  • the quality of the information presented in your paper

 

Bibliography and References

  • Within the text of the paper, you must properly cite each reference as you use it.
  • A citation is, of course, given for direct quotes.
  • A citation is also required when you paraphrase something from a source or if what you are saying is substantially inspired by a source.
  • You must give credit to your sources. To not do so is to plagiarize, and plagiarism violates the university's policy on academic integrity.
